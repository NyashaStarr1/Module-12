About

Investigative storytelling dedicated to uncovering truth, shifting perspective, and amplifying voices too often left unheard.

Our Mission

“Justice for the unheard. Light for the unseen.”

The Noir Cases is a global true crime podcast committed to shifting how stories are told, and whose stories are heard.

While mainstream media often focuses on sensationalized cases or those involving a narrow demographic, this platform exists to broaden that lens. Many cases affecting BIPOC communities are underreported, misrepresented, or ignored entirely, despite clear data showing disparities in coverage, investigation, and outcomes.

This podcast goes beyond retelling crime. It examines the systems behind it: policing, media, bias, accountability, and the societal structures that shape justice.

Through research, storytelling, and cultural context, The Noir Cases provides perspective, connecting individual cases to larger patterns and giving voice to stories that deserve visibility, understanding, and truth.

Nya Starr

Creator & Host — The Noir Cases

Nya Starr is the creator and host of The Noir Cases, where she examines true crime through a systemic, research-driven lens. With a background in Criminal Justice and sociology, her work blends academic insight with lived experience to challenge how crime stories are told, and who they center.

An Air Force veteran and longtime advocate, her work has always been rooted in confronting injustice and questioning institutional narratives. Through her platform, she amplifies marginalized voices and pushes beyond surface-level storytelling.
