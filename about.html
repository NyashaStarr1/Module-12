Our Mission

“Every case represents a person. Every person deserves justice.”

The Noir Cases is a global true crime podcast spotlighting marginalized voices. Hosted by Nya Starr, each episode explores cases affecting BIPOC communities—blending research, cultural context, and storytelling while bridging the gap between the criminal justice system and the communities it serves.

The mission goes beyond telling crime stories. It is about understanding the systems behind them—examining bias, disparities, and the structures that shape outcomes. While mainstream coverage often centers a narrow range of cases, many stories remain overlooked, underreported, or misrepresented.

Through data, lived experience, and critical analysis, The Noir Cases creates space for those voices. Not just to inform—but to shift perspective, challenge narratives, and expand what justice looks like.

Justice for the unheard. Light for the unseen.

Your Host

Nya Starr

CREATOR & HOST — THE NOIR CASES

Nya Starr is the creator and host of The Noir Cases, where she examines true crime through a systemic and research-driven lens. With a master’s degree in Criminal Justice and a foundation in sociology, she brings both academic depth and real-world perspective to every case.

An Air Force veteran and longtime advocate, her work challenges dominant narratives, amplifies marginalized voices, and pushes beyond surface-level storytelling to uncover the deeper forces shaping justice.
