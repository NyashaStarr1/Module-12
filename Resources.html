Resources

Essential resources for advocacy, support, and information related to criminal justice, missing persons, and systemic reform.

⚠

Emergency Support

If you are in immediate danger, call 911. For mental health crisis support, call 988. For text-based support, text HOME to 741741.

Section 1 — Advocacy & Legal

Organizations focused on legal reform, wrongful convictions, juvenile justice, and broader systemic accountability.

Innocence Project

Founded by Barry Scheck and Peter Neufeld, this organization works to exonerate wrongfully convicted people through DNA testing and reform.

Equal Justice Initiative

Founded by Bryan Stevenson, EJI challenges racial injustice, excessive punishment, and inequality within the criminal legal system.

International Justice Mission

A global organization working against violence, trafficking, and exploitation through legal advocacy and survivor-centered intervention.

Annie E. Casey Foundation — Juvenile Justice Advocacy

A reform-focused resource addressing youth incarceration, juvenile justice policy, and community-based alternatives for young people.

Section 2 — Survivor Support

Direct support resources for survivors, victims, and families seeking help, guidance, and recovery support.

RAINN

The largest anti-sexual violence organization in the United States, offering confidential support, education, and crisis resources.

SAKI Survivor Resources

Survivor-centered information and support related to sexual assault kit testing, justice access, and evidence reform.

National Human Trafficking Hotline

Connects victims and survivors of trafficking with reporting options, immediate help, and support resources in the U.S.

UNODC Human Trafficking

A United Nations resource focused on trafficking prevention, awareness, policy, and global anti-trafficking action.

Section 3 — Data & Media

Public data, research, and reporting tools that support awareness, accountability, and system-level understanding.

Bureau of Justice Statistics

Federal data and reports on crime, victims, incarceration, and criminal justice trends in the United States.

The Sentencing Project

Research and advocacy focused on incarceration, racial disparities, sentencing policy, and criminal justice reform.

Center for Policing Equity

Uses research and data to identify racial disparities and improve policing practices and public safety systems.

UNODC

Provides international crime, trafficking, and justice-related reports, research, and global data resources.

Section 4 — Missing Persons & Awareness

Organizations and reporting tools focused on awareness, family advocacy, and missing persons support.

National Center for Missing & Exploited Children

Provides prevention tools, case support, and awareness resources for missing and exploited children.

Black and Missing Foundation

Co-founded by Derrica and Natalie Wilson to address disparities in attention, response, and media coverage in missing persons cases.

MMIW Awareness

Advocacy and awareness resources addressing Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women and the systemic neglect surrounding these cases.

DOJ Missing Persons Action Center

Federal information and guidance for identifying, reporting, and responding to missing persons cases.

Section 5 — Community Support

Community-driven initiatives, prevention efforts, and advocacy resources shaped by impacted people and families.

Wear Orange

Inspired by Hadiya Pendleton, this movement was created by her loved ones and supporters to honor her life and promote gun violence prevention.

HEAR Foundation

A community-centered organization focused on healing, education, and support for people and communities impacted by violence.

Safe Kids Worldwide — Walk This Way

A prevention-focused initiative promoting pedestrian safety awareness for children, families, and local communities.

Color Of Change

A racial justice organization mobilizing Black communities to challenge inequality and push for systemic change.

Section 6 — Mental Health & Crisis

Mental health and wellness resources centered on access, culturally relevant care, and emotional support.

The Boris Lawrence Henson Foundation

Founded by Taraji P. Henson to reduce stigma and expand mental health support in Black communities.

Therapy for Black Girls

Founded by Dr. Joy Harden Bradford to connect Black women and girls with culturally competent mental health resources.

BEAM

Black Emotional and Mental Health Collective offers education, healing tools, and community-based mental health support.

Mental Health Coalition

Provides accessible wellness resources and support guides, including materials centered on Black mental health.
